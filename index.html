Patient Education

Common Teeth Problems Explained

Understanding your oral health is the first step to protecting it. Dr. Egbewole breaks down the conditions she treats most frequently.

Very Common
Preventive

Tooth Decay & Cavities

Cavities form when bacteria in the mouth produce acid that erodes tooth enamel. Left untreated, decay can reach the inner pulp and cause severe pain. Early treatment involves simple fillings — waiting can require root canal therapy or extraction.

Key signs: Toothache, sensitivity to sweets, visible holes Get Assessed
Adults 30+
Periodontal

Gum Disease (Periodontitis)

Gum disease begins as gingivitis — redness and bleeding when brushing — and can progress to periodontitis, where infection destroys the bone supporting your teeth. It is one of the leading causes of adult tooth loss and is entirely preventable with routine cleaning.

Key signs: Bleeding gums, bad breath, receding gum line Get Assessed
Very Common
Sensitivity

Tooth Sensitivity

A sharp, brief pain when consuming hot, cold, sweet, or acidic foods or drinks is a sign of exposed dentine — the inner layer of your tooth. Causes include enamel wear, gum recession, or cracked teeth. It is highly treatable once the root cause is identified.

Key signs: Sharp pain with temperature, discomfort when biting Get Assessed
Orthodontics
Alignment

Misaligned Teeth (Malocclusion)

Crowded, spaced, or misaligned teeth affect far more than aesthetics. They create areas that are difficult to clean, increasing decay risk. They also cause jaw strain, headaches, and uneven tooth wear. Braces and orthodontic treatment correct alignment at any age.

Key signs: Difficulty chewing, jaw discomfort, crooked appearance Get Assessed
Cosmetic
Cosmetic

Tooth Discolouration & Staining

Teeth can darken due to food and drink (tea, coffee, red wine), tobacco, ageing, or certain medications. Surface stains are removed by scaling and polishing; deeper discolouration responds to professional whitening. The cause determines the right treatment approach.

Key signs: Yellow, grey, or brown discolouration; uneven shading Get Assessed
Often Ignored
Oral Hygiene

Persistent Bad Breath (Halitosis)

Chronic bad breath is almost always a sign of an underlying dental condition — not just what you ate. Gum disease, untreated decay, dry mouth, or bacteria on the tongue are the most common culprits. A clinical diagnosis identifies the exact cause so targeted treatment can begin.

Key signs: Persistent odour not resolved by brushing or mints Get Assessed
